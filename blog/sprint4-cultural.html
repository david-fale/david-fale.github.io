Neuroplasticity

Understanding The Mind

23 February 2021

Why Understand Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is the ability for the brain to develop new connections. In simple terms the ability to learn. Understanding Neuroplasticity has shown to improve learning ability. Psychologist Carol Dweck talks about trials they have done with school childeren showing how teachning them about Neuroplasticity increases learning ability. This may be due to the fact the kids have more motivation as barrieres are broken down, or it could be they know how to properly tackle hard problems.

How can I utilise understanding Neuroplasticity?

I use what I have learned about neuroplasticity to help me learn smarter not harder. If I understand that the brain is malleable I can tackle difficult situations by breaking them down into smaller tasks and using more effort for those smaller tasks. I can use repetition to keep what I have learned in my long term memory. Thinking from another view point may be helpful in increasing my neuroplasticity as you have a better understanding on the challenge ahead of you. For example instead of trying to brute force a coding challenge I could take a step back from the problem and try look for an alternative path than the currnet strategy. This makes it more effective than just trying the same way as even if the new perspective doesnt work you have activated more of your brain trying that new angle of attack.

Ways to increase neuroplasticity

Neroplasticity can be increased by knowing the brain is able to be changed. Using repetition can strengthen the connections in your brain making it easier to recall the information you have learned. Using lions mane has shown to strengthen the myelin sheath which is responsilbe for improving neural conductivity in your brain (simmilar to how a rubber coating on a wire stops a current from leaving the wire). In simple terms stronger myelin sheath leads to better memory. here is a video that goes into good depth on the subject.

Growth Mindset

Growth mindset is understanding that you can learn anything with a good strategy and that the brain is not fixed in learning capacity. The only limit is yourself. This is important because knowing that you can learn anything means that when you come across a hard problem you can formulate a strategy to solve it instead of panicing and thinking you are not smart enough to solve it. I will be using this understanding of growth mind set to help me solve complex problems more efficently. Instead of trying to force it to work I can look at how I can break it down into small chunks and then solve it.
