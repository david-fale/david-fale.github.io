What is Emotional Intelligence

EQ (also known as Emotional intelligence) stands for Emotional Quotient which is your capability to manage your emotion and understand the emotions of others.

1. Self-awareness: the ability to recognise emotion as it happens. Facing what you are feeling instead of running away from it.
- Emotional awareness, recognising your emotions and how it affects others around you.
- Self-confidence, being assured of yourself and mental state.

2. Self-regulation: The way in which you handle your emotions.
- Self-control, managing your impulses. i.e. not giving into hedonism.
- Morality, upkeeping your honesty and integrity.
- Conscientiousness, taking responsibility for your actions
- Adaptability, changing with the circumstances.

3. Motivation: The level at which you can motivate yourself to achieve your goal.
- Achievement-driven, focusing on the long-term goal.
- Commitment, ability to stay with it through difficult situations.
- Initiative, taking action without being told to.

4. Empathy: The level that you can identify and understand the needs of other people.
- Understanding, be able to see how someone is feeling.
- Social skills: How well you can communicate to others

Difference Between IQ and EQ

IQ (intelligence quotient) is defined as the ability to understand and apply information to a given challenge. EQ (Emotional Quotient) is defined as the ability to identify and manage one’s emotions and others emotions around himself. IQ is more about understanding and utilising skills such as typing speed, problem solving, etc. Whereas EQ is more focused around understanding and affecting people (including one’s self).

Importance of Emotional Intelligence

Emotional intelligence has been a common trait found in High level executives. The ability to understand your own and others mental states makes it much easier to be able to traverse a workplace environment. Understanding yourself is important as if you do not address certain issues within yourself, they can surface later on in more negative ways. This has been referred to as understanding your shadow in Jungian archetypes. It is also important to understand other people’s mental states as you will be able to interact with them better. By having good EQ you can stop miss communication and disagreements from arising.
